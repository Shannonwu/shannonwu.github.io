第五章 莎士比亚戏剧中的欲念

· 2019-06-27 · # 翻译 # 文学课 # 精神分析

节译自Ehsan Azari, Lacan and the Destiny of Literature

第五章 莎士比亚戏剧中的欲念

Chapter 5 Shakespeare’s Theatre of Desire

From our theoretical inquiry into desire as well as our exploration of Lacan’s modes of reading literature and critical methodologies in the previous chapters, we are now able to examine desire in Shakespeare. This chapter is divided into two parts in order to investigate the structure and textual modes of both masculine and feminine desires in a selection of key Shakespearean texts. In light of Lacan’s seminar on Hamlet, the first part will scrutinize the literary articulation of masculine desire in that play, with special focus on some of Lacan’s important theoretical points that have been mostly omitted in post- Lacanian literary debates.

从我们在先前章节中对于欲念的理论性探索，与拉康式的文学解读与批评方法论的研究，我们现在即可检视莎士比亚文本中的欲念。本章分为两部分，以探讨选定的莎士比亚文本中男性与女性欲念的结构和文本图式。本章分为两部分，以探讨选定的莎士比亚文本中男性与女性欲念的结构和文本图式。受拉康讨论班《哈姆莱特》启发，第一部分将细查的是该剧本中男性欲念的文学链接，主要针对的是那些被后拉康文论讨论所忽略的理论要点。

I wish to consider those important points that make this play the epitome of a masculine desire in deep crisis. This crisis will be followed by a reading of Hamlet’s soliloquies – a metonymical vehicle for sustaining the mourning that is constitutive of his desire. Then we will explain Hamlet’s interpersonal swing between the object in and of desire, the theme of the hour of the Other, and Hamlet’s conversation with Gertrude, as the dénoue- ment of a chaotic desire. This section ends with a study of the play-within-the-play, Hamlet’s ‘mouse trap’, not as a traditionally Oedipal allegory, but as textual evidence for the return of Hamlet’s desire. In the second part of the chapter, the focus will be on paradigms of feminine desire in Coriolanus, Macbeth and The Merchant of Venice. The latter texts will allow us to demonstrate the multiplicity of feminine desire, and the interdependence of masculine and feminine desires not as two opposite categories but as two types of unconscious preference. We will examine two kinds of the phallic mother in Coriolanus and Macbeth. We will conclude with a survey of the fluidity of feminine desire in The Merchant of Venice.

我希望能考察那些促使这个剧本成为男性欲望深度危机的典型的要点。这些危机随着对于哈姆雷特独白的阅读而来——一种换喻性手段以绵延其欲望基础的哀悼。随后，我们将阐释哈姆雷特人际间针对客体中和客体自身的欲念的延宕；彼者时间的主题；作为混沌欲念的结局，哈姆雷特与乔特鲁德的对话。这一部分以针对剧中剧——哈姆雷特的《捕鼠器》——的研究收尾，它并不是传统意义上的俄狄浦斯隐喻，而是作为哈姆雷特的欲念回归的文本证据。本章的第二部分将关注在《科利奥兰纳斯》、《麦克白》和《威尼斯商人》中的女性欲望图式。接下来的文本将允许我们演示女性欲望的多重性，进而说明男性与女性欲望的依存性，并不是两种对立的类别，而是两种无意识的倾向。我们将检视在《科利奥兰纳斯》和《麦克白》中的石祖母亲。我们将以《威尼斯商人》中女性欲望的流动性报告作结。

These plays are selected because: first, they offer a fertile ground for Lacan’s theory of desire in which literary experience converges on psychoanalytic experiment; and second, some of the texts display prototypes and models of theoretical material that Lacan was to develop later. This also reveals that Lacan seems always keen to see Hamlet like any other literary text, representing a pre-existing truth about desire. From this vantage point, Lacan and Shakespeare make an ideal match, as the former postulates a psychoanalytic theoretical position for which the second has already produced textual illustrations.

这些剧本之所以入选，是因为：一来，他们为拉康的欲望理论在文学与精神分析经验的交汇提供了肥沃的土壤；二来，其中部分文本展示了拉康晚期创造的基础模型与图示的理论材料。这些文本也揭示了拉康相较于其他文本对哈姆雷特的偏爱，再现了关涉欲望的既存真理。正是在这样的有利地位上，拉康与莎士比亚构成一对理想组合，前者假设为精神分析的理论地点，为后者制造了文本说明。
